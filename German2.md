# German Notes

## Useful phrases

- Ich liebe dich. = I love you.
- Freut mich. = Nice to meet you / pleased to meet you.
- Wie heißt du? = What is your name?  
	Informal: used with friends or people your age.
- Wie heißen Sie? = What is your name?  
	Formal: used with strangers, teachers, or in polite situations.
- Wie geht's dir? = How are you?  
	Informal.
- Das ist mein Handy. = This is my phone.
- Was ist das? = What is that?
- Ich heiße Christopher. = My name is Christopher.

## Pronunciation reminders

- a sounds like an open "ah" sound.
- o sounds like a rounded "oh" sound.
- u sounds like a long "oo" sound.
- h in many words is soft or silent depending on the word.
- z is often pronounced like "ts".

## Diphthongs

Diphthongs are vowel combinations where the sound changes across the letters.

- au
- eu
- ai / ei

## Capitalization rules

In German, nouns are always capitalized.

Examples:

- der Kaffee
- der Tee
- der Tisch = the table
- die Tasche = the bag
- die Tasse = the cup
- das Auto
- das Handy

## Articles: der, die, das

German has three main articles:

- der = masculine
- die = feminine
- das = neuter

Examples:

- der Kaffee
- der Tee
- der Tisch
- die Tasche
- die Tasse
- das Auto
- das Handy

## Word meanings

- jung = young
- sehr = very

## Alphabet tips

Helpful English-style pronunciations:

- V = "fau"
- W = "veh"
- B = "beh"
- M = "em"
- T = "teh"

Examples:

- VW = "fau-veh"
- BMW = "beh-em-veh"

## Umlauts

German uses umlauts: ä, ö, ü.

They change the vowel sound and can change the meaning of a word.

## Quick correction examples

- heisse / heiße = I am called / I heat
- ich heiße = my name is / I am called
